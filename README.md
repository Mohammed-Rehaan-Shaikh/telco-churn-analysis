# Telco Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn for a telecom company using Python and Tableau. The goal is to identify customer groups with higher churn risk and understand the business impact of customer churn.

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Plotly
- Tableau

## Dataset
The dataset used in this project is the Telco Customer Churn dataset from Kaggle.

## Business Questions
- What is the overall churn rate?
- Which contract types have the highest churn?
- Which payment methods are linked with higher churn?
- Which customer groups are more likely to churn?
- Which services are connected with customer retention?
- How much monthly revenue is at risk due to churn?

## Project Workflow
1. Loaded the dataset
2. Cleaned and prepared the data
3. Performed exploratory data analysis using Python
4. Created KPI calculations
5. Built Tableau dashboards
6. Summarized business insights and recommendations

## Key Insights
- Month-to-month contract customers showed higher churn risk.
- Customers with shorter tenure were more likely to churn.
- Electronic check payment users had higher churn behavior.
- Fiber optic internet customers showed higher churn compared to other internet service groups.
- Customers without online security or tech support were more likely to churn.
- Churned customers represented monthly revenue at risk for the business.

## Recommendations
- Create retention offers for month-to-month customers.
- Focus onboarding efforts on new customers with short tenure.
- Review customer experience for electronic check payment users.
- Investigate possible service quality issues for fiber optic customers.
- Promote support services such as online security and tech support.
- Track monthly revenue at risk regularly.

## Dashboard
The Tableau dashboard was created to show:
- Churn KPIs
- Churn distribution
- Churn by contract type
- Churn by payment method
- Churn by tenure group
- Revenue at risk
- Customer risk segments

## Conclusion
This project shows how customer churn analysis can help a telecom company identify risky customer segments and make better retention decisions.
